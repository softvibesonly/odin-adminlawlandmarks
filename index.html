The Following Are a List of Landmark Cases in Canadian Administrative Law (non-exhaustive)

1: Canada (Minister of Citizenship and Immigration) v. Vavilov

citation: 2019 SCC 65, [2019] SCJ No 65 (QL)

A picture of a Russian Passport (which Vavilov did not have lol)

    Important Notes:
  • Not every tribunal required to give decisions
  • The duty of procedural fairness in admin law is “eminently variable”
  • Where reasons are required, they are the starting point for administrative decision-makers ADMs to show that their decisions were reasonable
      • Some Reasons To Give Reasons:
    1. Transparency
    2. Justification
    3. Intelligibility

2:Doré v. Barreau du Québec

citation: 2012 SCC 12, [2012] 1 SCR 395

A picture of the Fleurdelisé

    Important Notes:
  • Normally, when judicially reviewing a discretionary admin decision made by adjudicator, the decision is reviewed for its reasonableness
  • But when a Charter issue is involved, should the test be the same?
  • An adjudicated admin decision is not the same as a law which can, in theory, be objectively justified by the state
  • In the Charter context, reasonableness analysis is one that centers on proportionality
      • We look at:
    1. Does the decision interfere with the Charter right no more than is necessary, given statutory objectives?
    2. Does it reflect a proper balance of the mandate with Charter protections?
